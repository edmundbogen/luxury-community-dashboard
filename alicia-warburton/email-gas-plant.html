From My Perspective

Having relocated from the New York area and watched St. Petersburg's transformation over the past decade, I understand why this development commands attention from sophisticated investors. This isn't just about 86 acres—it's about whether St. Petersburg can execute the kind of thoughtful urban development that attracts long-term capital.

The history here matters. The displaced Gas Plant community's story is one reason this development faces such scrutiny. But for investors, the critical question is execution: Can the city balance community commitments with creating a district that drives sustained property value appreciation across downtown?

The answer will shape St. Petersburg's trajectory for the next two decades. I'm tracking this closely—and I'd welcome the opportunity to discuss what it means for your investment strategy.
