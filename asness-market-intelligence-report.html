Your Current Market

Royal Palm Yacht & Country Club

Boca Raton, Florida — The benchmark against which both options should be measured.

Where It Is

Royal Palm Yacht & Country Club is a 450-acre gated community on the Intracoastal Waterway in east Boca Raton. Located less than one mile from the beach and adjacent to the Boca Raton Resort & Club (Waldorf Astoria), it sits approximately 20 miles south of West Palm Beach and 25 miles south of Palm Beach Island.

The Community

Royal Palm is home to 749 homesites—299 interior, 221 golf course, and 229 waterfront/Intracoastal—with estates ranging from 2,500 to 20,000+ square feet, with current listings from approximately $2M to $34.75M and a median listing price near $11.97M. Approximately 60–65% of residents live year-round, making it a true community rather than a seasonal outpost.

The club features a Jack Nicklaus Signature 18-hole championship golf course, a private marina with direct Intracoastal-to-ocean access (no fixed bridges), six Har-Tru tennis courts, and world-class croquet facilities. Membership is invitation-only, requiring two member sponsors: full golf membership is $125,000 initiation plus approximately $18,000/year in dues; social membership is $50,000 plus approximately $12,000/year. Membership is not required to own property.

Why It Matters as a Baseline

Royal Palm sits in Boca Raton's lower-millage tax jurisdiction (approximately 16.7 mills versus 21.2 for West Palm Beach and 14.9 for Palm Beach Island). At the price points you are considering, this tax differential becomes material. At $20M assessed value, Royal Palm saves roughly $90,000/year in property taxes versus an equivalent-value West Palm Beach condominium.

Beyond taxes, Royal Palm offers something neither of the other two markets can match: you own the land, the structure, and have significantly more control over your improvements than a condominium. The Royal Palm Improvement Association (RPIA) serves as the community's homeowners' association, enforcing architectural standards and building maintenance requirements to protect property values—but this is a fundamentally different level of oversight than a condo board governing your unit. You also have significantly more architectural freedom than Palm Beach Island's ARCOM review process. Contemporary and transitional designs are welcomed alongside Mediterranean estates.

Royal Palm offers the strongest combination of waterfront luxury, architectural freedom, and year-round community feel at the ultra-luxury tier in South Florida. It is the standard against which the other two options should be measured.

Option A — Single-Family Estates

Palm Beach Island

North of Sloan's Curve — The North End and Estate Section of one of the most exclusive real estate markets in the world.

Where It Is

Palm Beach is a barrier island approximately 16 miles long, separated from West Palm Beach by the Intracoastal Waterway. Sloan's Curve is the landmark bend on South Ocean Boulevard near the southern boundary of the Estate Section. North of Sloan's Curve encompasses the Estate Section and North End—the most prestigious residential enclave on the island, characterized by legacy oceanfront estates on lots ranging from 0.3 to 6+ acres. This is approximately 25 miles north of Royal Palm and directly across the Intracoastal from the West Palm Beach condominium corridor.

Market Character

This is an ultra-exclusive market defined by generational wealth, legacy properties, and a social season (November through April) intertwined with cultural institutions, Worth Avenue, and the Wellington Equestrian Festival. Approximately 85% of buyers originate from the Northeast, and 83%+ of transactions are all-cash. An estimated 20–40% of ultra-luxury deals above $20M never appear on MLS, which is why broker relationships and off-market access are essential in this price tier.

Architecture & Design Standards

Mediterranean Revival remains the dominant and most favored style with Palm Beach's Architectural Commission (ARCOM). British Colonial, Bermuda, and British West Indies styles are equally well-received. Georgian, Classical, and Neoclassical designs gain approval readily. Contemporary designs face significant hurdles in the approval process. The 2025 trend is lighter Mediterranean Revival with simplified arches, refined textures, and neutral palettes.

Estate Section homes typically range from 7,000 to 20,000+ square feet on lots of 0.3 to 6 acres, with 4–7 bedrooms standard. The defining market trend is the teardown-rebuild cycle: buyers purchase existing estates specifically to demolish and build custom.

Carrying Costs

Property Taxes

Palm Beach Island's combined millage rate is approximately 14.9 mills. Without homestead exemption: ~$80K on $5M, ~$160K on $10M, ~$400K on $25M assessed value. Florida's Save Our Homes 3% annual assessment cap is enormously valuable over time.

Insurance

$10M home: $60K–$130K/year. $20M home: $120K–$250K/year. Hurricane deductibles: 2–10% of insured value. NFIP flood coverage caps at $250K—private flood insurance is essential. The market is stabilizing: 73 rate decrease filings and 17 new carriers since 2022.

Regulatory, Environmental & Security

ARCOM — The Critical Gatekeeper

Palm Beach's Architectural Commission (ARCOM) reviews all exterior modifications visible from public rights-of-way. No building permit issues without ARCOM approval. The 7-member board holds broad subjective authority, and multiple re-study requests are routine. Fees are modest ($2,750 for a major application, capped at $75K), but the timeline is the true cost: 3–6+ months for approval alone, with total project timelines of 24–36+ months.

Flood Risk & Sea Level

Most of Palm Beach Island falls within FEMA Zone AE (high risk). FEMA updated flood maps effective December 20, 2024—16,269 parcels face greater risk, with 3,413 parcels seeing base flood elevation increases of 3+ feet. The Southeast Florida Climate Compact projects 10–17 inches of sea level rise by 2040 and 21–54 inches by 2070. New construction must be approximately 2.5 feet above road grade.

Demolition & Seasonal Constraints

Town demolition permits include restrictions: work may not commence until after May 1st, with application cutoffs that apply. Turtle nesting season (March–October) restricts oceanfront and seawall work. These constraints can add multi-month delays and increase carrying costs.

Presidential Security Impact

South Ocean Boulevard experiences closures when the President is in residence (typically Friday mornings). A permanent 1-nautical-mile flight restriction is in effect 24/7, expanding to 30 miles during presidential visits. The security zone does not extend north into the Estate Section, though bridge closures can affect the entire island. Market impact has been overwhelmingly positive: post-election $10M+ closings surged 125% YOY.

Option B — Luxury Condominiums

West Palm Beach Waterfront

Flagler Drive Corridor — 16+ luxury condominium projects reshaping the West Palm Beach skyline.

Where It Is

West Palm Beach's luxury condominium corridor runs along Flagler Drive on the western shore of the Intracoastal Waterway, directly facing Palm Beach Island. Nearly all 16+ active projects are concentrated along this waterfront stretch. The Town of Palm Beach is a 5-minute bridge crossing east. Downtown West Palm Beach—now branded "Wall Street South" following the relocation of Goldman Sachs, Citadel, and dozens of financial firms—is immediately to the west. Brightline high-speed rail connects WPB to Miami in approximately 75 minutes.

The Lifestyle Proposition

West Palm Beach condominiums offer a fundamentally different value proposition than either Royal Palm or Palm Beach Island estates. This is a lock-and-leave lifestyle: 24/7 concierge, valet, building security, and maintained common areas. For buyers splitting time between residences or traveling frequently, the convenience is genuine. Branded residences (Ritz-Carlton, Mr. C/Cipriani) provide hotel-level services including dining, spa, housekeeping, and concierge.

East-facing Intracoastal views from these towers are permanently protected by Palm Beach Island's height restrictions—no future construction can obstruct the sightlines. The proximity to Palm Beach island culture (Worth Avenue, Royal Poinciana Plaza, The Breakers) combines with the new financial corridor energy downtown.

Carrying Costs & Tax Analysis

Property Taxes

West Palm Beach's combined millage rate of approximately 21.2 mills translates to an effective property tax rate of roughly 2.12% of assessed value—meaningfully higher than Palm Beach Island (14.9 mills) or Boca Raton (16.7 mills).

At your price range: At $10M with no homestead exemption: ~$212,000/year. At $20M: ~$424,000/year. Florida's homestead exemption ($50,722) saves only ~$1,075/year at luxury price points. However, the Save Our Homes 3% annual assessment cap is enormously valuable long-term.

HOA & Monthly Costs

Monthly HOA fees in these buildings range from approximately $4,000 to $14,000+ depending on the building, unit size, and amenity package. Branded residences (Ritz-Carlton, Mr. C) may carry additional service fees. These costs cover exterior maintenance, building insurance, staff, common areas, and amenities—but represent a significant ongoing expense not present in single-family ownership.

The Tax Advantage That Matters Most

Regardless of which South Florida market you choose, the decisive financial advantage remains Florida's zero state income tax. For high-income buyers relocating from the Northeast, the elimination of state and city income taxes can save $267,000+ annually on $2M income—far exceeding any property tax differential between these three markets.

Market Considerations

As with any active development market, buyers should be aware that several condominium projects in the West Palm Beach corridor have experienced construction-related disputes, design changes, and timeline delays. This is not unusual in a market with this volume of simultaneous construction activity. We strongly recommend thorough due diligence on any specific building under consideration, including review of all available legal filings, association documents, and independent engineering assessments.

Additionally, Florida's post-2021 milestone inspection requirements (SB 4-D, effective December 2024) mandate structural inspections at 25 years for buildings within 3 miles of the coast and 30 years for all others. New construction provides 25–30 years before these inspections apply, but buyers in completed buildings should verify inspection status and any reserve assessments.

Buyer Protection

Due Diligence Checklist

Consolidated from our research across all three markets. The specific items that apply depend on whether you pursue a Palm Beach Island estate or a West Palm Beach condominium.

For Palm Beach Island Estates

Entitlement & Buildability

  • Confirm zoning district, nonconformities, and historic/landmark status before making an offer.
  • Hire an ARCOM-experienced architect before purchasing any property you plan to modify.
  • Run a seasonality-aware construction schedule: demolition timing must align with town rules or face multi-month idle carrying costs.
  • For oceanfront sites, confirm Coastal Construction Control Line (CCCL) implications and state-level coastal permitting requirements.

Physical Risk & Environmental

  • Obtain a certified elevation certificate and verify flood zone under December 2024 updated FEMA maps.
  • Model sea level rise exposure using NOAA/FEMA tools as a capital expenditure and livability variable.
  • Inspect seawalls on Intracoastal properties (replacement cost: $100K–$300K+, with turtle season restrictions).
  • Assess septic systems—many island properties still use them.

Financial & Insurance

  • Obtain bindable insurance quotes early. Deductibles can reach $200K–$1M.
  • Underwrite construction at Palm Beach Island costs ($800–$1,500+/SF), not mainland benchmarks.
  • Stress-test resale assumptions with 15–25% adverse scenarios on North End quarterly data.

Market Execution

  • Segment by liquidity tier: under $30M, $30M–$75M, and $75M+ are fundamentally different markets with different buyer pools.
  • Best negotiation leverage: June–September (5–6% off asking). Properties 60+ days on market year-round.
  • Ensure your broker has active relationships on Palm Beach Island: 20–40% of ultra-luxury deals are transacted off-market.

For West Palm Beach Condominiums

Legal & Financial Documents

  • Full condominium prospectus/public offering statement, declaration, bylaws, and amendments.
  • Current year budget, last 2–3 years of audited financials, reserve schedules, and pending special assessments.
  • For branded projects (Ritz-Carlton, Mr. C): all brand-license and service agreements; confirm separation of developer vs. brand liabilities.
  • Written disclosure of any pending litigation (association vs. developer, construction claims, insurance disputes).

Tax & Cost Analysis

  • Parcel-level tax estimator for the specific unit; compute full millage (city + county + school + special districts).
  • Confirm whether the unit is within city limits or unincorporated Palm Beach County (affects millage rates).
  • Model total carrying costs: HOA + insurance (HO-6 + flood) + property tax + any brand service fees.

Building & Construction

  • Certificate of occupancy status, punch-list completion, warranty administrator contact.
  • Inquire about any known water-intrusion or façade remediation items.
  • Elevator maintenance contract and uptime history.
  • Sound attenuation and glazing specs (critical near bridges and arterials).

Views & Market Position

  • Request stacking plan, setbacks, and survey/site plan for view-premium units.
  • Identify pipeline projects within the immediate view corridor.
  • Visit during construction hours to assess noise, dust, and traffic from adjacent projects.
  • Confirm rental restrictions: minimum lease terms, frequency limits, application/approval process.

Key Questions for Developers & Brokers

  • Current unsold inventory by line and floor (not just "% sold") and developer pricing policy for remaining units.
  • Projected HOA fee at stabilization and dominant line-items (staffing, insurance, reserves, amenities).
  • Parking allocation, EV charging capacity, guest parking, and valet obligations.
  • For hotel-integrated buildings: shared cost structure and governance between hotel and residential.
  • Deposit schedule, assignability policy, and rescission/termination framework.
